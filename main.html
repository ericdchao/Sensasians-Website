Who We Are

Sensasians is Wash U's premier Asian interest A Cappella group. Founded in 2009, we sing in English and various Asian languages. Known as the singing pandas on campus, we specialize in bilingual mashups and more!

Our Founders

The Sensasians were founded in 2009 by alumni Lisa Ma and Phoebe Tran. Being avid music lovers, they realized that despite Washington University's bolstering A Cappella community, there wasn't a group that specialized in East-Asian music. With a goal to blend East-Asian music with pop, rock, and everything else, they gathered their talented karaoke friends and founded the Wash U Sensasians. Hailing the panda as our mascot, our goal is to mix up music from different cultures, make good music, and have a blast doing it.

What We Do

Despite being the youngest of the 12 A Cappella groups on campus, The Sensasians are always kept on their feet performing for a huge variety of events and locations. During the regular school year, we perform for various University-wide functions, and make appearances at almost every Asian cultural group event on campus. We travel to a different location every year for an annual joint concert with other members of Quad-A (Asian American A Cappella Association). We also compete in ICCA's each year in addition to our semester-ly concerts.
